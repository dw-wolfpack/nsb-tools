Freelance Rate Calculator for Software Engineers

Software engineers often command higher freelance rates due to specialized skills. This page provides defaults and guidance tailored to devs.

Last updated: February 27, 2025

What this tool does

The freelance rate calculator gives hourly, day, and project rates from desired income, expenses, utilization, and buffer. Use the link below to open with software-engineer defaults. Not tax advice. Not AI-powered.

When to use it

  • Setting or revising your freelance rate as a software engineer
  • Checking if your rate covers income goals and expenses

Inputs explained

  • Desired income, expenses – After-tax goal and annual expenses.
  • Billable hours, vacation, utilization – 65 to 75% utilization common for devs. See utilization.
  • Buffer % – 15 to 25% for scope creep and unpaid discovery. See buffer.

How to use it

  1. Open the calculator (link below). Adjust inputs if needed. Click Calculate.
  2. Review hourly, day rate, and project minimums.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming 100% utilization (70% is often optimistic)
  • Skipping buffer for scope creep

FAQ

What utilization do most software freelancers see?

65 to 75% is common. Non-billable time includes sales, admin, learning, and context switching.

Should I price by hour or project?

Both. Use hourly for T&M; use project minimums (10h, 20h, 40h) for fixed-scope to avoid underquoting short engagements.

How does this differ from a data engineer or designer rate?

Same formula. Adjust inputs for your niche. Data engineers and senior devs often sit in similar or higher ranges.
