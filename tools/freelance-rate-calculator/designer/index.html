Freelance Rate Calculator for Designers

UX, UI, brand, and product designers use the same rate math: income goal, expenses, billable hours, utilization, buffer.

Last updated: February 27, 2025

What this tool does

The freelance rate calculator gives hourly, day, and project rates from desired income, expenses, utilization, and buffer. Use the link below to open with designer defaults. Not tax advice. Not AI-powered.

When to use it

  • Setting or revising your freelance rate as a designer
  • Pricing fixed-fee brand or product design projects (use project minimums as anchors)

Inputs explained

  • Desired income, expenses – After-tax goal. Designers often target $80k to $120k.
  • Billable hours, utilization – Design has revision cycles; 60 to 70% utilization is realistic. Buffer 20 to 25%.

How to use it

  1. Open the calculator (link below). Adjust inputs if needed. Click Calculate.
  2. Review hourly, day rate, and project minimums (10h, 20h, 40h).

Common mistakes

  • Using 100% utilization (design has more non-billable client feedback)
  • Underquoting fixed-fee projects (use minimums)

FAQ

Should designers price by hour or project?

Both. Hourly for retainers and T&M; project for fixed deliverables. Use minimums to avoid underquoting.

How does utilization differ for designers?

Design often has more revision cycles and client back-and-forth. 60 to 70% is realistic.

What about design systems or rebrand work?

Same formula. Estimate total hours for the project and use the hourly from the calculator.
