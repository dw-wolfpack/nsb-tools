Break-even Calculator for Service Businesses

Service businesses (consulting, agencies, freelancing) have fixed costs (rent, salaries, tools) and variable cost per "unit" (e.g. per project or per hour delivered).

Last updated: February 27, 2025

What this tool does

The break-even calculator in service mode tells you how many units (projects, hours, or jobs) you need to cover fixed costs. You enter fixed costs per month, variable cost per unit, and price per unit. Use the link below to open the calculator with service defaults. Educational only; not financial advice.

When to use it

  • Planning a consulting or agency and want to know how many engagements to break even
  • Pricing services and checking if you can cover fixed costs
  • Comparing scenarios (e.g. different price or variable cost)

Inputs explained

  • Fixed costs per month – Rent, salaries, software, marketing.
  • Variable cost per unit – Subcontractors, materials, travel per engagement.
  • Price per unit – Average project fee or blended hourly rate.

How to use it

  1. Open the calculator (link below). Select Service mode.
  2. Enter fixed costs, variable cost per unit, and price per unit. Click Calculate.
  3. Review break-even units and revenue.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing monthly and annual numbers
  • Setting price less than or equal to variable cost

FAQ

What is a "unit" for services?

Whatever you sell: project, retainer month, billed hour. Use your average.

Variable cost for pure services?

Often low (tools, subcontractors). Can be $0 if you deliver solo.

Is this financial advice?

No. Educational only.
