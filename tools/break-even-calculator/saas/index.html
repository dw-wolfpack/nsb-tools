Break-even Calculator for SaaS

SaaS uses MRR and customers instead of units. Fixed costs (salaries, infra, tools), gross margin (revenue minus COGS), and ARPA (average revenue per account per month) determine break-even.

Last updated: February 27, 2025

What this tool does

The break-even calculator in SaaS mode shows how many customers and how much ARPA and gross margin you need to cover fixed costs. Use the link below to open the calculator with SaaS defaults. Educational only; not financial advice.

When to use it

  • Modeling SaaS and seeing how many customers or how much MRR you need to break even
  • Checking if your unit economics support your burn

Inputs explained

  • Fixed costs per month – Salaries, cloud, tools, office.
  • Gross margin % – 70 to 85% for software. See gross margin.
  • ARPA – Average revenue per account per month. Blend of plans if you have tiers. See ARPA.

How to use it

  1. Open the calculator (link below). Select SaaS mode.
  2. Enter fixed costs, gross margin, ARPA. Click Calculate. Review break-even customers and MRR.

Common mistakes

  • Using revenue instead of gross margin
  • Mixing monthly and annual numbers

FAQ

What is ARPA?

Average revenue per account per month. MRR divided by customer count.

Gross margin for SaaS?

Revenue minus hosting, support, payment fees. Typically 70 to 85%.
