Loan Payoff Calculator for Business Loans

SBA, term loans, and equipment financing use amortization. Model payoff and see how extra principal payments shorten the term and reduce interest.

Last updated: February 27, 2025

What this tool does

The loan payoff calculator shows months to payoff, total interest, and amortization. For business loans (SBA, term, equipment), enter principal, rate, and monthly P&I; add extra to see savings. Use the link below to open with business-loan defaults. Educational only; not financial advice.

When to use it

  • Modeling SBA 7a, term loan, or equipment financing payoff
  • Seeing how extra principal payments reduce interest and shorten term

Inputs explained

  • Principal – Loan balance. SBA 7a: 8 to 11%, 10 to 25 years. Term: 6 to 12%, 3 to 7 years. See principal.
  • Monthly payment – From lender. Extra payments go to principal.

How to use it

  1. Open the calculator (link below). Enter principal, rate, monthly payment. Optionally add extra.
  2. Click Calculate. Review payoff and amortization.

Common mistakes

  • Using annual payment instead of monthly
  • Ignoring prepayment terms (some loans have fees for early payoff)

FAQ

Does this work for SBA loans?

Yes. Use the principal, rate, and monthly P&I. SBA 7a and 504 are amortizing.

What about lines of credit?

Lines of credit are revolving. Use for term loan portions only.

Is this financial advice?

No. Educational only. Consult a lender or advisor.
