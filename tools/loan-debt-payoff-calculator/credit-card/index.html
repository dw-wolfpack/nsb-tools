Loan Payoff Calculator for Credit Card Debt

Credit cards often charge 15 to 25% APR. Extra payments make a big difference. This calculator shows months to payoff and total interest.

Typical inputs for credit cards

Principal $3k to $15k. APR 18 to 24% common. Minimum payment usually 1 to 2% of balance or a floor (e.g. $25). Add $50 to $200 extra to accelerate.

Examples

Example 1: $6k at 19%, $150/mo minimum. Payoff ~5 years, ~$3.5k interest. $75 extra: payoff ~2.5 years, save ~$1.5k.

Example 2: $10k at 22%, $200/mo. Payoff ~7 years. $100 extra cuts ~2.5 years.

Example 3: Snowball: pay minimum on all, put extra on smallest balance first for quick wins.

FAQ

Does this account for minimum payment changes?

It assumes fixed payment. Real minimums can change as balance drops.

Should I use avalanche or snowball?

Avalanche (highest rate first) saves more interest. Snowball (smallest balance first) can boost motivation.

Is this financial advice?
