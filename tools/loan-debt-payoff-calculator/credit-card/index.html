Loan Payoff Calculator for Credit Card Debt

Credit cards often charge 15 to 25% APR. Extra payments make a big difference. This calculator shows months to payoff and total interest.

Last updated: February 27, 2025

What this tool does

The loan payoff calculator shows months to payoff and total interest for credit card debt. Enter principal, APR, and monthly payment; add extra to see savings. Use the link below to open with credit-card defaults. Educational only; not financial advice.

When to use it

  • Planning payoff for credit card debt
  • Seeing how extra payment shortens payoff and saves interest

Inputs explained

  • Principal – Current balance. APR 18 to 24% common. See principal and interest rate.
  • Monthly payment – Minimum (often 1 to 2% of balance or a floor). Add $50 to $200 extra.

How to use it

  1. Open the calculator (link below). Enter principal, APR, monthly payment. Optionally add extra.
  2. Click Calculate. Review payoff timeline and interest.

Common mistakes

  • Tool assumes fixed payment; real minimums can change as balance drops

FAQ

Does this account for minimum payment changes?

It assumes fixed payment. Real minimums can change as balance drops.

Should I use avalanche or snowball?

Avalanche (highest rate first) saves more interest. Snowball (smallest balance first) can boost motivation.
