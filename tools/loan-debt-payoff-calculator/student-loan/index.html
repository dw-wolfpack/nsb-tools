Loan Payoff Calculator for Student Loans

Model payoff timeline and total interest for federal or private student loans. Add extra payments to see how much faster you pay off and how much interest you save.

Last updated: February 27, 2025

What this tool does

The loan payoff calculator shows months to payoff, total interest, and an amortization table. For student loans, use principal, rate, and monthly payment; add extra payment to see interest savings. Use the link below to open with student-loan defaults. Educational only; not financial advice.

When to use it

  • Planning payoff for federal or private student loans
  • Seeing how extra payment shortens the term and saves interest

Inputs explained

  • Principal – Current balance. Federal rates 4 to 7%; private 5 to 12%. See principal and interest rate.
  • Monthly payment – Minimum or fixed payment. Try $50 to $200 extra.

How to use it

  1. Open the calculator (link below). Enter principal, rate, monthly payment. Optionally add extra payment.
  2. Click Calculate. Review payoff timeline and total interest.

Common mistakes

  • Entering annual payment instead of monthly
  • Income-driven plans change over time; tool assumes fixed payment
